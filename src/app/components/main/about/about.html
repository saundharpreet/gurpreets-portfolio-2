What do I do again?

Good question. I’m Gurpreet Kaur, a Ph.D. student in Animal Science. Basically, I spend my days obsessing over soil, microbes, and how to make forage crops happier and healthier. And yes, it is as nerdy and fascinating as it sounds.

Originally from a small village in Punjab, India, I grew up around farms and dairy animals. My family’s love for gardening inspired me to dive deep into agriculture. Now I’m here in the US with the Nutrient Management Spear Program, trying to figure out how microbes can secretly save the world, one field at a time.

Quick Facts

  • Education: MSc in Microbiology (Hons.), Punjab Agricultural University, India
  • Thesis: Effect of biofertilizer and organic fertilizer on soil health and cowpea growth
  • Awards: R P Sethi Memorial Grant for highest GPA in Microbiology, Merit certificate for GPA above 8.0/10
  • Advisor: Quirine Ketterings
